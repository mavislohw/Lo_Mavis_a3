about

Like every “art” kid, I grew up scribbling on walls. By the time I moved out of my childhood home, you could see scribbles all over our hallways (yes it was a pain to paint over, trust me).

I never thought a creative career was possible, but I always found myself back in art and design. Whether it was making a presentation unnecessarily over designed or painting banners for school concerts, I couldn’t help myself but to be drawn to it.

I started my journey in design like many others, in graphic design. It started with yearbook pages then to Instagram posts and event posters. I was obsessed with every detail from typography to composition to colours.

Design felt like home.

But, I did not fully realize that until I almost died in a car crash (very real and very scary experience). It was then that I made the decision to accept the offer from the only design program I applied to (the rest was criminology and psychology, what a switch!). So here I am now at the University of Waterloo!

I major in Global Business and Digital Arts, minoring in Business, Entrepreneurship and Technology.

Through my program and my friends, I fell in love with product and branding design. It is the perfect balance of creativity x problem solving x intention.

When I am not head down designing on my laptop, I love indulging in my endless hobbies and learning new things. When I can, I like to travel and take a piece of everywhere I go with me.

I am constantly restless and looking for a new project to sink my teeth in. I am also driven by the belief that I can learn anything and everything.
